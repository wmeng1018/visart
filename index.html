Multimodal profiling of chromatin states with NTT-seq

Abstract: Chromatin states are functionally defined by a complex combination of histone modifications, transcription factor binding, DNA accessibility, and other factors, However, most current single-cell-resolution methods are unable to measure more than one aspect of chromatin state in a single experiment, limiting our ability to accurately measure chromatin states. Here, we introduce nanobody-tethered transposition followed by sequencing (NTT-seq), a new assay capable of measuring the genome-wide presence of multiple histone modifications and protein-DNA binding sites at single-cell resolution. We applied bulk- and single-cell NTT-seq to generate high-resolution multimodal maps of chromatin states in cells of the human immune system, and further extend NTT-seq to enable simultaneous profiling of cell-surface protein expression alongside multimodal chromatin states.
